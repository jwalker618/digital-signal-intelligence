Introducing Digital Signal Intelligence

A new information substrate that enables deterministic, autonomous underwriting.

The Core Thesis

Insurance underwriting requires understanding two properties of an organisation:

1
How well it is managed
2
How it behaves over time

Traditional underwriting attempts to infer these from self-reported documents—subjective, inconsistent, and non-verifiable.

DSI replaces them with observable digital signals.

Why Now? The PageRank Precedent

DSI is not experimental. It uses the same network-based principles that underpin modern search and recommendation systems.

1. THE PROBLEM

Pre-1998 Search

Search engines ranked pages by counting self-reported keywords, resulting in spam and manipulation.

2. THE BREAKTHROUGH

PageRank (1998)

Google introduced a new information substrate: quality inferred from citation patterns and network authority.

3. THE PARALLEL (DSI)

Digital Signal Intelligence

DSI applies the same principle: risk quality is inferred from observable digital signals.

Key Insight: Absence as a Signal

Where traditional underwriting treats missing data as unknown, DSI inverts this: absence is itself a signal. A financial institution without security headers, a tech company without a careers page, a regulated entity without certification documentation—these absences are measurable negative indicators.

Confidence as a First-Class Metric

Every signal carries a confidence score (0-1) reflecting data availability, source reliability, and signal freshness. The system knows when it knows—and critically, when it does not.

1. Network Authority

Signals from an entity's position within networks of trust, partnership, and transaction.

  • Client/partner citations
  • Certification relationships
  • Industry body memberships
  • Supplier network quality

2. Technical Infrastructure

Observable technical implementation revealing operational discipline.

  • TLS/SSL configuration
  • DNS security (DNSSEC, SPF, DKIM)
  • Software currency
  • Hosting infrastructure

3. Corporate Footprint

Entity's own digital presence signals maturity and stability.

  • Website structure & recency
  • Leadership visibility
  • Hiring activity
  • Policy documentation

4. Behaviour

Observable operational behaviour over time.

  • Change patterns
  • Incident response
  • Regulatory engagement
  • Customer interaction

5. Public Record

Regulatory filings, litigation, and official records.

  • Corporate registries
  • Litigation records
  • IP filings
  • Breach disclosures

6. Structured Data

Authoritative third-party provider data.

  • Credit ratings
  • ESG scores
  • Market data
  • Classification society records

7. Direct Inquiry (Optional)

Minimal structured questions when external signals insufficient.

  • 5-10 questions max per model
  • Binary or categorical only
  • Verifiable where possible
  • Used only to fill critical gaps

Signals are Externally Observable, Machine-Readable, and Verifiable

This enables deterministic scoring without self-reported input. Each signal is normalised to 0-100 and carries a confidence score reflecting availability, source reliability, and freshness.
