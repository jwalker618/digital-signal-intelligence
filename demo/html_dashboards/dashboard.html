Key Insight: What DSI Cannot Detect

Honest Limitation: True Zero-Day Vulnerabilities

The MOVEit case demonstrates that DSI cannot predict unknown vulnerabilities. This is correct behavior—no methodology can predict zero-days. The solution is to price residual software supply chain risk into all vendor dependencies and enforce concentration limits.

Core Principles

1

External Observability

Only use signals that are externally observable through standard reconnaissance, public records, or third-party data feeds. Never rely on internal attestations alone.

2

Conservative Scoring

When uncertain whether a signal was observable, assume it was not. Better to miss a true positive than create false confidence.

3

Honest Limitations

Explicitly acknowledge what DSI cannot detect. Zero-day vulnerabilities, specific internal configurations, and attack targeting cannot be predicted.

4

Continuous Validation

Regularly test DSI against historical incidents to verify predictive validity. Adjust weights based on empirical performance.
