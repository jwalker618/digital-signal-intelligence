What happened?

On February 21, 2024, Change Healthcare—a major U.S. healthcare payment processor and subsidiary of UnitedHealth Group—was hit by a ransomware attack that became the largest healthcare data breach in U.S. history, affecting 190–192.7 million individuals. The attack crippled billing, insurance claims, and pharmacy operations nationwide, disrupting patient care and causing severe financial strain on hospitals and providers.

  • Critical healthcare infrastructure

ZERO-DAY EXPLOITATIONMay 2023

MOVEit / Progress Software

US Technology - 600M USD Revenue - 3,000 Employees

$10B across all victims

What happened?

In late May 2023, a zero-day vulnerability (CVE-2023-34362) in MOVEit Transfer, a managed file transfer solution by Progress Software, was exploited in one of the largest supply-chain cyberattacks in history. The breach impacted over 2,700 organizations and exposed the personal data of 93–96 million individuals, including sensitive PII such as Social Security numbers and banking details. Victims included major corporations (BBC, British Airways, Boots), government agencies (U.S. Department of Energy, Louisiana DMV), and healthcare providers. Estimated financial impact exceeded $9–15 billion globally.

Traditional Underwriting View

  • Specific tooling not interrogated

What happened?

On June 18–19, 2024, CDK Global—a leading SaaS provider for automotive dealerships—was hit by a ransomware attack that forced the company to shut down its IT systems nationwide, disrupting operations at over 15,000 dealerships across North America. The outage lasted nearly two weeks, leaving dealerships unable to process sales, financing, payroll, or service scheduling, and forcing them to revert to manual paperwork. Industry-wide revenue losses were estimated at $600 million in the first two weeks, with some analysts projecting $1 billion total impact.

  • 15,000 dealership customers
