Three Layer Assessment

Decoupling Complexity for Absolute Clarity

Traditional underwriting conflates risk quality, exposure magnitude, and loss propensity into a single subjective judgement. DSI separates them into three independent, measurable layers.

Risk Layer

Utilising a PageRank-inspired digital signal citation framework to assess organisational quality.

Exposure Layer

A deterministic inference of an organisation's exposure magnitude without self-reported schedules.

Loss Layer

Correlating real-time signal behaviours with historical loss patterns to infer propensity.

Independent layers enable calibration, auditability, and agentic execution.
Risk Analysis

The Risk Layer assesses organisational quality across six dimensions:

1. Governance Stability
Board composition, C-suite tenure, audit committee structure
2. Network Authority
Backlink quality, partner relationships, citation patterns
3. Technical Posture
Security controls, infrastructure maturity, patch cadence
4. Operational Discipline
Hiring patterns, headcount stability, employee sentiment
5. Financial Health
Credit signals, filing patterns, revenue trajectory
6. Regulatory Standing
Compliance history, enforcement actions, disclosure quality
Risk Layer Detail
Exposure Shadow Layer

The Exposure Layer infers organisational scale and complexity without self-reported schedules:

1. Exposure Band
Classification of exposure magnitude using direct observables (employee count, office footprint), high-confidence proxies (revenue estimates, technology spend), and inferred signals (subsidiary count, geographic dispersion).
2. Complexity Category
Measures organisational complexity: geographic dispersion, subsidiary count, technology heterogeneity, and regulatory footprint. Outputs: Simple, Moderate, Complex, Highly Complex.

Outputs ranges with confidence (e.g., "$75M–$180M at 78% confidence") instead of false-precision point estimates.

Exposure Layer Detail
Loss Correlation Layer

The Loss Layer infers propensity from behavioural patterns correlated with historical loss data:

1. Loss Propensity Score (0–100)
Measures likelihood of loss frequency based on behavioural and structural patterns. Signals: governance changes, hiring freeze, security posture deterioration.
2. Severity Propensity Score (0–100)
Measures likelihood of high-severity outcomes. Signals: concentration risk, complexity, regulatory exposure.
3. Loss Trend Band
Classifies trend direction: Improving, Stable, Deteriorating. Based on signal velocity over 90/180/365 day windows.
4. Cohort Assignment
Entities are grouped into signal-derived peer cohorts for outlier detection and pricing calibration.

Digital Signal Intelligence

The Core Thesis

Traditional underwriting attempts to assess two fundamental properties of an organisation:

  1. How well it is managed — governance, controls, operational discipline
  2. How it behaves over time — stability, trajectory, responsiveness to change

These properties are currently inferred from self-reported documents: submissions, spreadsheets, questionnaires, and broker narratives. This approach is fundamentally flawed because the inputs are subjective, inconsistent, and impossible to verify at scale.

DSI replaces them with observable digital signals.

Digital Signal Intelligence defines a new information substrate where both properties — management quality and behavioural patterns — are derived from externally observable, machine-readable signals that exist independently of the insured's self-reporting.

This enables deterministic, autonomous underwriting: consistent, auditable, and scalable to any volume without proportional increase in human effort.
