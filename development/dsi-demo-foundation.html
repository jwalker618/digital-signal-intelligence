Home / The PageRank Precedent

The PageRank Revolution

The original substrate shift in search

The Challenge

Early search engines relied on keywords (self-reported content), leading to spam, manipulation, and irrelevance.

The Insight

Page and Brin realised that a page's value is revealed by its citations—who links to it and the authority of those links.

The Rule

A node trusted by trusted sources must itself be trustworthy. Authority emerges from network structure, not content.

The Proof

By prioritising the Digital Citation Network, Google captured 92% of global search market share.

DSI Parallel: Insurance can infer risk quality from digital footprints at scale.

Section 5 of 5

Why Three Layers?

Traditional Conflation

Traditional underwriting merges risk quality, exposure magnitude, and loss propensity into a single subjective judgement.

Analytical Failure

This conflation prevents calibration, automation, and auditability. No one knows which factor drove the decision.

DSI Separation

DSI separates underwriting into three independent, measurable layers: Risk, Exposure, and Loss.

This decomposition is essential for determinism, calibration, and agentic execution.

Section 1 of 5

The Risk Layer

Composite Risk Score (0–1000)

Weighted aggregation of signals across all seven canonical categories.

Risk Tier (1–5)

Composite scores map deterministically to risk tiers with predefined decision logic.

Confidence Score (0–1)

Reflects data availability, source reliability, and signal freshness.

Overrides & Modifiers

Deterministic rules adjust tiers when specific signal conditions are met.

The Risk Layer answers: "How well is this organisation managed?"

Section 2 of 5

The Exposure Shadow Layer

Exposure Band

Classification of exposure magnitude using direct observables, high-confidence proxies, and inferred signals.

Complexity Category

Measures geographic dispersion, subsidiary count, technology heterogeneity, and regulatory footprint.

Honest Uncertainty

Outputs ranges with confidence (e.g., "$75M–$180M at 78% confidence") instead of false-precision point estimates.

The Exposure Layer answers: "How large and complex is the insurable exposure?"

Section 3 of 5

The Loss Correlation Layer

Loss Propensity (0–100)

Measures likelihood of loss frequency based on behavioural and structural patterns.

Severity Propensity (0–100)

Measures likelihood of high-severity outcomes.

Loss Trend Band

Classifies trend direction: improving, stable, deteriorating.

Cohort Assignment

Entities grouped into signal-derived peer cohorts for outlier detection and pricing.

The Loss Layer answers: "How likely is a loss event, and how severe might it be?"

Section 4 of 5

Combining the Three Layers

Independent Inputs

Each layer is assessed independently using the same underlying signal infrastructure.

Unified Pricing Decision

Risk, Exposure, and Loss combine deterministically to produce a premium and decision.

Full Audit Trail

Every step, score, and modifier is captured at the atomic grain for auditability.

This architecture enables deterministic, agentic underwriting at scale.
